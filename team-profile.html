Team Profile

Every member of our team has taken a few online tests so that we can learn about ourselves and optimise our team functionality. A personality test, a learning test, and a test of the team members choice have been completed and we used the information gathered to help determine the best way our team could operate.

We all started by sourcing the 16 Personalities Myers-Briggs Test. The test tells us about our personality in 4 areas just like the Myers-Briggs Test, however It has the addition of one more factor called ‘Identity’. This factor underpins all the other scales by determining how confident we are in our decisions.

16 Personalities Myers-Briggs Test

Adam’s Results: With the 16Personalities.com analysis Adam was described as an "Architect" which has the acronym INTJ. The Architect personality type is one that only makes up 2% of the population. Architects are proud of the knowledge that they gain and like to share It with others. They believe that anything is possible with enough effort and thought. When Adam reflects on his results, he can conclude that it does describe him well. Bearing this in mind, he realises that he must concentrate a little more on working as part of a team rather than taking on the project on his own. Adam understands that his positive traits will also surface in a team-based role and that the work will speak for itself.

Allan’s Results: From Allan’s testing, a low extraversion score is shown over all his tests, meaning he is less likely to put himself into social situations. He is also more likely to sway towards situations that are familiar to him rather than trying new experiences. People with this ISTP personality type are natural makers, enjoying moving from project to project and building the useful and the superfluous for the fun of it. They also regularly learn from their environment as they go.

While Allan finds the potential insights from these different tests interesting, he does find himself skeptical of their ability to predict his behaviours in professional settings and when working in teams. The breakdown for Allan’s test results are as follows:

Deon’s Results: The results of this test show that he is best suited as an advocate diplomat. This has the character code of INFJ-T. He is there to lubricate the gears for a team so that everything keeps running smoothly. However, Deon realises that he should take care to put effort into the work that he does and not just to helping others. This personality type leads to a person who is a problem solver. They try to help others in need and usually want to eradicate the route of the problem while they are at it.

Kylie’s Results: The results of Kylie’s Myers-Briggs personality test are INTP. This is a logician character. The basic aspects of this personality type are of a person who is quite logical, does not use emotions as a basis for understanding or making decisions and is quite often a loner. Their minds can often easily spot patterns and problems.

Tomas’ Results: According to this test, Tomas is an INFP-T mediator. This means he is very idealistic, yet flexible and open-minded. It also means that he is creative, and that he can combine his creativity and open-minded nature to be a very level-headed individual. The study says that Tomas has a hard-working and dedicated nature when challenges pop up that would deter most. However, the test also mentions some of the negative characteristics of such a person. For example, they can be impractical. Also being too idealistic can set them up for disappointment and therefore their morale can deteriorate. Tomas agrees with aspects of the test results but not all of them (which is expected). He believes that he is a hard worker and that he can also think too simply sometimes as we are not in an ideal world. However, Tomas is against the motion that he cannot see the bigger picture. Contrary to that, he thinks that he often only gets through situations by seeing the end goal.

Overall, Tomas would say that the test is moderately accurate and so he must take it into consideration for teamwork. He should look for personalities that harmonise with his own yet would add substance for a good team. As a person who is idealistic, Tomas needs someone who can be realistic to bring his ideas back down to Earth. Also, as a rather dominant character he would need to be in a team without too many like-minded people or the project could get dragged in too many directions.

Conclusion We can draw ideas from the data we have received to create a harmonious and effective team. As in any team, we require certain characteristics for a well-rounded unit. Some of these properties can include having a team leader who takes control and organise. We need some people who like to show their creative flare, and others who like to plod on through to just get the work done. We can set up our team in a way that utilises our strengths and minimises our weaknesses. If we look at the data, we can see that Adam, being an architect, has great assets for leading a team and guiding others. Meanwhile Allan could be a valuable team mechanic. He can start sections, setting up the groundwork and then move onto another goal. Through Kylie’s logical thinking and Deon’s eye for problem fixing we could capitalise early on issues that may arise. This could allow us to quickly move past obstacles so that progress is not hindered. Tomas, on the other hand, could help navigate through dilemma’s by listening to two viewpoints and trying to negotiate an effective middle ground.

Learning Test Adam, Deon, and Tomas are predominantly visual learners, while Allan is a kinesthetic learner. Kylie is also a visual learner, but she, like Allan, learns through a kinesthetic approach as well. Visual learners use material that is right in front of them to help them understand something. They can appreciate graphical data and information in a textbook or on a whiteboard. It is a very traditional approach to gaining knowledge but for some of our team, this is the best way that they retain information. They can often enjoy drawing something out to use as a study tool that they can refer to. Demonstrations can be very important for visual learners as well as other visual representations of instruction.

We also have two people in our group who learn through physical activities and ‘doing’ things. These characteristics of a kinesthetic learner can be aided by a hands-on approach. Sometimes ‘acting out’ or simulating ideas can help with retainment and learning.

We also have people who will learn somethings through auditory mediums. Deon and Tomas have this as a way that they can affectively learn.

Conclusion: Having varied ways of learning throughout the team can be of great benefit as we can easily look at problems from all angles. If information is portrayed in a certain manner, we can be sure that someone will be able to understand due to our variety of learning preferences. However, due to us all learning in different ways, we must make sure that everyone is comfortable with the new information as there is no guarantee that everyone will understand.

Kylie’s Big 5 Personality Test: Kylie does not agree with some of her results. She disagrees with the idea that she is an angry or self-conscious person. The test has given Kylie high scores for both, as well as scoring her highly in some respects that she specifically picked as less applicable to herself. It has also given her quite high scores in intelligence, creativity, morality, and cooperation.

Tomas’ Creativity Test: Tomas’ final test was a creativity test. The following test describes him as having a complex and abstract mind. This means that Tomas can produce abnormal concepts from ideas or information. The complex side of the test means that he can handle large quantities of information at once and be able to manipulate and manage the information well. It also claims that Tomas lacks the ability to make connections. This means unless something has an original apparent connection, he may struggle to link the two concepts together.

Tomas is not sure how much he agrees with this test. Normally, he would say that he is able to hand and manipulate information, but he is not convinced that he is not able to see new connections between ideas. Tomas would say that this is one of his strengths. The inaccuracy of the test could be purely down to the limited number of questions as creativity is such a complicated characteristic. Tomas also says that he probably would not use this test to identify good potential fits for team members whom he can work with. He wants to take a few more creativity tests to see what type of creativity he should be looking for in a team.

Conclusion: Adam could use his newfound knowledge about his decision-making skills to influence his team in the correct way. He knows that he can trust his instincts and that he will be right most of the time. However, for the most part, these final tests wouldn’t allow our team to develop any decisions on how to work in a team as 3 out of the 5 tests gave our team members similar information to what they already had, and Tomas didn’t agree with the information he learned in his creativity test.

Ideal Jobs

Each member of 6Teen has a good and solid idea of where they might like to take their future IT career paths. Deon, Kylie, Tomas, and Adam were all focused specifically on coding and programming positions. All of which require many more years of experience and training, as well as previous experience in similar positions in the case of Adam, Kylie and Deon’s chosen positions.

Allan was our outlier in his ideal position of Helpdesk or IT Support. Allan’s chosen career path involves a lot of customer service skills as well as an in-depth knowledge of the software (in this particular case, Windows) or the ability to find solutions and workarounds for his clients. Being a customer service oriented position his technical knowledge, while important, would not be enough without the ability to communicate well with clients, supervisors, and any personel working under him.

Similarly, Adam’s chosen position as a software developer also requires experience in customer service as well as the required coding knowledge in java and .Net. His chosen position also requires prior experience in a similar organisation as well as the ability to multitask and work on several projects at once.

Deon’s chosen position was a software engineer for VR and hardware. This position requires an extensive knowledge base and years of experience. Although not all jobs require this, having more experience will lead to a greater chance of obtaining such a role. Deon’s chosen role will require experience in game design, as well as specifically working with VR technology, interactions between humans and computers, and the use of software specific to the industry such as the game engines Unity and Unreal.

Kylie’s chosen position of mobile game developer also focuses on game development and the use of the Unity engine alongside learning other specific skills such as animation, coding in C#, and working specifically with 3D technologies, whether that was in games or other technologies. Her chosen position also requires previous experience developing a game that was successfully launched in the app or play store.

Tomas chose a junior developer’s position as his ideal position. This position requires the skills to develop, test, and maintain software. His chosen position also requires knowledge of several coding languages including C#, CSS and, Javascript. Customer service skills and good communication are also listed as required for such a role, as it is for many in the industry.

On the surface all of us except Allan have very similar goals and career paths in mind; however once we look deeper there is a lot of overlap in each of our positions and some large differences as well. Allan, Adam and Tomas all chose careers where customer service skills were a must. Both Deon and Kylie have chosen positions that require previous game development and experience in the same game engines. Despite the shared experience, the actual positions require knowledge of different platforms and hardware.

Offering similarities.

IT Support was a small subtheme of our group’s goals in learning IT, with Tomas and Allan requiring these soft skills as a prominent part of their job. Programming skills were more across the board of us all, in some cases the chosen position requiring upward of 4 different languages required from their chosen position. Allan’s ideal position remains out largest outlier, requiring learning the technical specifics of one program or system and all its functionalities. Each of our ideal career positions require previous experience working in similar companies and in some cases proving commercial success with those companies.

Despite each position being some aspect of IT and four of the five being in very similar sounding careers it is very clear that each of these positions are far away from each other in terms of our chosen career routes. While there is a lot of overlap between the positions each of them are unique and the differences are quite large. It will be very interesting to see the directions they may end up taking us in the long term.
